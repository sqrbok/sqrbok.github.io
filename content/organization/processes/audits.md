# Quality Audits

Quality audits are systematic, independent examinations to determine whether quality activities and results comply with planned arrangements and whether these arrangements are implemented effectively.

---

## Overview

Quality audits serve to:
- Verify process compliance
- Identify improvement opportunities
- Ensure standards conformance
- Provide objective evidence of quality
- Drive corrective and preventive actions

---

## Types of Audits

### Internal Audits
- Conducted by organization's own staff
- First-party audits
- Focus on process improvement
- Regular schedule (quarterly, annually)

### External Audits
- Conducted by external parties
- Second-party (customer) or third-party (certification body)
- Focus on compliance and certification
- As required by contracts or standards

### Process Audits
- Examine adherence to defined processes
- Check process documentation
- Review work products and records

### Product Audits
- Examine delivered software artifacts
- Verify requirements compliance
- Check documentation completeness

---

## Audit Process

### Planning
- Define audit scope and objectives
- Select audit team
- Review documentation
- Prepare audit checklist
- Schedule audit activities

### Execution
- Conduct opening meeting
- Review documents and records
- Interview participants
- Observe activities
- Collect evidence
- Conduct closing meeting

### Reporting
- Document findings
- Classify nonconformances (major/minor)
- Identify observations and opportunities
- Issue audit report

### Follow-up
- Develop corrective action plans
- Implement corrections
- Verify effectiveness
- Close audit findings

---

## Audit Checklists

Common audit areas:
- Requirements management
- Design control
- Code reviews
- Testing procedures
- Configuration management
- Change control
- Documentation
- Training and competence

---

## Audit Findings

Classification:
- **Critical nonconformance**: Major process failure
- **Major nonconformance**: Significant process gap
- **Minor nonconformance**: Isolated process lapse
- **Observation**: Opportunity for improvement
- **Best practice**: Exemplary implementation

---

## Corrective and Preventive Actions

**Corrective Actions** (fix the problem):
- Immediate containment
- Root cause analysis
- Permanent solution
- Verification of effectiveness

**Preventive Actions** (prevent recurrence):
- Identify potential problems
- Eliminate root causes
- Implement preventive measures
- Monitor for effectiveness

---

## Audit Best Practices

1. **Focus on process, not people** - Audit is not personal
2. **Be objective and evidence-based** - Facts, not opinions
3. **Use sampling appropriately** - Representative samples
4. **Stay within scope** - Don't scope creep during audit
5. **Be professional and respectful** - Collaborative, not adversarial
6. **Follow up systematically** - Verify corrective actions
7. **Learn and improve** - Use audits for improvement

---

## Common Audit Pitfalls

- Auditing to "catch" violations
- Excessive documentation requirements
- Ignoring context and practicality
- Not following up on findings
- Audit fatigue from over-auditing
